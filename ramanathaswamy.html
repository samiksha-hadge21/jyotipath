About Place:

The shrine is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is one of the twelve jyotirlinga temples in India. Ramanathaswamy temple is known for its imposing structure, majestic towers, intricate sculptural works and corridors, making it an architectural marvel. The main deity worshipped in the temple is in the form of lingam.


History:

History of Rameshwaram Temple - Origins, Historical SignificanceThe Sri Ramanathaswamy Temple in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, is steeped in mythology and history. The temple is a pilgrimage site for Shaivites and Vaishnavites. Mythological history According to legend, Lord Rama built a bridge of floating stones to rescue his wife Sita from Ravana, the demon king of Lanka. Rama prayed to Shiva to absolve his sins for killing Ravana, a Brahmin. Rama sent Hanuman to bring the Shivlinga from Mount Kailash. When Hanuman took longer than expected, Sita made a Shivling out of sand from the seashore. Construction history The temple was built by rulers starting in the 12th century. The temple was expanded during the Nayak period in the late 16th century. The temple's current design is believed to have been built in the 17th century.

Architecture:

The Sri Ramanathaswamy Temple in Rameshwaram, India is known for its Dravidian and Vijayanagara architectural styles. The temple's architecture includes high walls, large towers, and long corridors. Walls The temple has high walls, called madils, on all four sides The walls are made of granite and are marked with intricate carvings Towers The temple has large towers called gopurams at the east and west ends The eastern gopuram is 126 feet tall and has nine levels Corridors The temple has long corridors, called prakarams, that run between huge colonnades The outer corridor is said to be the longest in the world The corridors are made of sandstone pillars, beams, and high ceilings


Best Time To Visit

Rameshwaram In Winter (October to February) The best time to visit Rameshwaram is between October to February. During these months, the temperature usually drops, ranging between 20-30 degrees Celsius. The days are chilly, and evenings are pleasant. It's also the time when major festivals are celebrated, and you can find homestays at budget-friendly rates. Rameshwaram In Monsoon (July to September) These months are perfect for those who enjoy rain and solitude amidst lush green surroundings. Rameshwaram experiences temperatures ranging between 29-34 degrees Celsius during these months, with humid days and drizzle. There are ample accommodation options, including homestays, hotels, and resorts, offering budget-friendly rates for a peaceful vacation. Rameshwaram In Summers (March to June) March to June are the hottest months in Rameshwaram and also when the town is crowded with pilgrims from across the country. Various processions take place during this time. The weather ranges between 26 to 40 degrees Celsius. Days are hot, but evenings are pleasant. Budget travelers can still plan a trip and book stays at discounted rates.



How To Get Thier


By Road : Rameshwaram is well-connected by road to various cities in Tamil Nadu and nearby states. You can choose from government-operated buses, private buses, or even luxury coaches. Here are the key routes: From Chennai: The most common route is via Chennai. You can catch a bus from Chennai’s Koyambedu Bus Terminus, which is approximately 570 kilometers away from Rameshwaram. The journey takes around 10-12 hours, There are multiple bus operators providing services on this route, including SETC (State Express Transport Corporation) and private operators.

By train: The Rameshwaram Railway Station is well-connected to major cities in South India. Traveling by train is not only economical but also provides picturesque views of the countryside. Here’s how to reach Rameshwaram by train: From Chennai: You can catch a train to Rameshwaram from Chennai Egmore Railway Station. The train journey offers scenic views and is an excellent way to experience the local culture. The train journey takes approximately 12-14 hours.

By air: Though there’s no airport in Rameshwaram, the nearest airport is Madurai Airport, which is well-connected to major Indian cities. After arriving at Madurai Airport, you can continue your journey to Rameshwaram by road. Here’s how to reach Rameshwaram by flight: From major cities: You can book a flight to Madurai Airport from major Indian cities like Chennai, Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, and Hyderabad.
