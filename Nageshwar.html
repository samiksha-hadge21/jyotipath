About Place:

Nestled in the ancient city of Dwarka, the Nageshwar Jyotirlinga is a revered pilgrimage site dedicated to Lord Shiva. One of the 12 Jyotirlingas in India, this holy temple is steeped in mythological tales and legends. It is believed that the Nageshwar Jyotirlinga represents the infinite nature of Lord Shiva and offers protection from all evils. The temple is famous for its striking architecture, featuring a massive 25-metre tall statue of Lord Shiva, which is a sight to behold.


History:

The Nageshwar Jyotirlinga temple in Dwarka, Gujarat, India is steeped in legends of devotion and divine intervention. Legends The demon Daruka A demon named Daruka tortured a devotee of Lord Shiva named Supriya for refusing to teach him how to worship Shiva. Supriya's faith and chants of "Om Namaha Shivay" impressed Shiva, who appeared and defeated Daruka. Shiva then chose to reside at the site as a Jyotirlinga. The dwarf sages A group of dwarf sages called the Balakhilyas worshipped Shiva in Darukavana. Shiva disguised himself as a nude ascetic to test their devotion. The sages' wives were attracted to Shiva and left their husbands, which enraged the sages. They cursed Shiva to lose his linga, which caused earthquakes and other disruptions. Shiva took back his linga at the request of Brahma and Vishnu. Architecture:

The total height of the temple is 110' above Ground's level and it has many beautiful elements like arches, rounded flute shaped columns, lotus themed capitals, jails made of pure marble etc., the whole structure is interspersed with swastika and Kalash which are traditional Hindu worship signage.


Best Time To Visit

Best Time to Visit If you wish to set out for a holy pilgrimage of the Nageshwar temple, the winter months are ideal; i.e. the months between October to February. During that time the weather of Nageshwar area remains pretty pleasant with cool breezes. However, for travelers who do not mind the piercing heat of the summers, Nageshwar temple tour is an all-season tour that has profound spiritual significance on every full moon night. Nageshwar in Summers Summers in Nageshwar area around Dwarka start from the end of February and continue till July first week. The summer weather here is hot and very humid and sticky. Maximum temperatures range between 33 degrees centigrade to 40 degree centigrade and minimum temperatures are near 22 degrees centigrade. Travelling in daytime especially noon time is not recommended during this time. Nageshwar in Monsoons Monsoons in Nageshwar creep in around July and it continues pouring till September. Monsoons here are characterized by light showers to medium range showers and it doesn’t rain very heavily. August receives the highest rainfall when compared to the other monsoon months with the average monthly recorded rainfall being around 250mm. Humidity levels rise tremendously during the monsoon months making one feel quite uncomfortable. Wearing only light and airy fabrics is recommended. Nageshwar in Winters Winter in Nageshwar set in around beginning of October and continue till February. Winters here are pleasant, cool and not bitingly cold making them travel-friendly. Average maximum winter temperatures hovers around 25 degrees centigrade to 28 degree centigrade and minimum temperatures is around 12 degrees centigrade.
