Jyoti Path

Temples



Somnath Temple - Patan

The temple is said to have been built in the 7th century by the moon god, Chandra, to worship Lord Shiva. The temple is located at the confluence of the Kapila, Hiran, and Saraswati rivers, a sacred site known as a Triveni Sangam.

Mallikarjuna Temple - Srisailam

According to Hindu scriptures, the Jyotirlinga originated when Goddess Parvati playfully closed Lord Shiva's eyes, causing darkness to fall over the universe. To restore balance, Lord Shiva performed penance on Mount Kailash.

Mahakaleshwar Temple - ujjain

The lingam at the Mahakala is believed to be swayambhu (born of itself) deriving currents of power shakti from within itself as against the other images and lingams which are ritually established and invested with mantra-shakti.




omkareshwar temple - khandwa

According to one legend, the island on which the temple sits is shaped like the Sanskrit letter "Om". This is because the deity of the Vindhya mountain range, Vindya, created a lingam made of sand and clay in the shape of "Om" while worshipping Lord Shiva.

Baidyanath Temple - Deoghar

The temple was built in the 8th century by Puran Mal, an ancestor of the Nagavanshi dynasty. The temple's origin is also connected to the legend of Ravana, who offered nine of his heads to Shiva as penance. When Shiva appeared to accept the offering, Ravana asked to take the "Kamna Linga" to Lanka.

Bhimashankar Temple - Pune

According to one legend, a demon named Bhima performed penance to please Lord Brahma, who then appeared as Lord Shiva and reduced Bhima to ashes. The place where Lord Shiva appeared is believed to be the location of the Shivalinga.




Ramanathaswamy Temple - Rameswaram

According to the epic Ramayana, Lord Rama wanted to worship Lord Shiva after defeating the demon king Ravana. He sent Hanuman to Mount Kailash to bring a lingam, but Hanuman was delayed. In the meantime, Sita made a lingam out of sand, which is now known as the Ramalingam.

Nageshwar Temple-Dwarka

it was here that a demon called Daaruka imprisoned a Shiva devotee called Supriya. The chants of 'Om Namaha Shivay' by Supriya invoked Lord Shiva who arrived here and vanquished the demon. A self-manifested Shivalinga appeared here and is prayed to this date.

Kashi Vishwanath Temple- varanasi

According to legend, the Jyotirlinga appeared in Varanasi when Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva argued about their supremacy. Shiva manifested as a column of light, and Vishnu and Brahma tried to find its source. Vishnu took the form of a boar and tracked the column underground, while Brahma took the form of a swan and searched the heavens. Brahma claimed to have found the column's summit, but Shiva punished him by cutting off his fifth head and cursing him.




Trimbakeshwar Temple - Nashik

According to legend, the Trimbakeshwar Jyotirlinga originated when Lord Shiva established himself on earth after Gautama Rishi worshipped him

Kedarnath Temple -Rudraprayag

According to Hindu mythology, the Pandavas built the temple after seeking forgiveness from Lord Shiva for killing their relatives in the Mahabharata war. Shiva disguised himself as a bull to avoid the Pandavas, but they chased him until he disappeared in Kedarnath. Bhima, one of the Pandavas, held onto the bull's hump, which remained in place after the bull disappeared. The lingam in the temple is shaped like a hump.

Ghrishneshwar Temple -Aurangabad

The temple is said to have originated from a legend about a woman named Kusuma who worshipped a Shiva Lingam under a tamarind tree. One day, a honeybee poured honey on the lingam, which transformed into Lord Shiva. The temple was built on the spot where the event took place.
