About Place:

The intricately carved honey-coloured Somnath temple on the western edge of the state is believed to be the place where the first of the twelve holy jyotirlingas emerged in India – a spot where Shiva appeared as a fiery column of light. The temples lies at the meeting of Kapila, Hiran and Sarasvati rivers and the waves of the Arabian Sea ebb and flow touching the shore on which it is constructed. The ancient temple’s timeline can be traced from 649 BC but is believed to be older than that. The present form was reconstructed in 1951. Colourful dioramas of theShiva story line the north side of the temple garden, though it’s hard to see them through the hazy glass. A one-hour sound-and-light show in Amitabh Bachchan’s baritone highlights the temple nightly at 7.45pm.


Brief History:

It’s said that Somraj (the moon god) first built a temple in Somnath, made of gold; this was rebuilt by Ravana in silver, by Krishna in wood and by Bhimdev in stone. The current serene, symmetrical structure was built to traditional designs on the original coastal site: it’s painted a creamy colour and boasts a little fine sculpture. The large, black Shiva lingam at its heart is one of the 12 most sacred Shiva shrines, known as jyotirlinga. A description of the temple by Al-Biruni, an Arab traveller, was so glowing that it prompted a visit in 1024 by a most unwelcome tourist – the legendary looter Mahmud of Ghazni from Afghanistan. At that time, the temple was so wealthy that it had 300 musicians, 500 dancing girls and even 300 barbers. Mahmud of Ghazni took the town and temple after a two-day battle in which it’s said 70,000 defenders died. Having stripped the temple of its fabulous wealth, Mahmud destroyed it. So began a pattern of destruction and rebuilding that continued forcenturies. The temple was again razed in 1297, 1394 and finally in 1706 by Aurangzeb, the Mughal ruler. After that, the temple wasn’t rebuilt until 1950.


Best Time To Visit

Somnath in Winters (November to February) Somnath is a pilgrimage destination in Gujaratand is famous for housing one of the 12 holy Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. The town is thronged with pilgrims and devotees and holds spiritual significance among many. The best time to visit Somnath is between the months of November to February when the weather is cool. The average temperature ranges between 12-28 degrees Celsius, and stays and homestays are available to book at slightly costly rates as Somnathis thronged with pilgrims during these months. Somnath in Monsoon (July to September) These are the months when Somnath receives heavy rainfall, along with thunderstorms. The city is drenched, and there are high chances of the formation of cyclones during the monsoon. The average temperatures during these months usually range between 22-30 degrees Celsius. There are ample stays to choose from, ranging from homestays to hotels and resorts, and one can enjoy a peaceful vacation at budget-friendly rates. Somnath in Summers (March to June) Considered to be the hottest time of the year, March to June are the months when Somnath experiences high heat and humidity. The average temperature ranges between 32 to 42 degrees Celsius, with days usually being hot and unpleasant. Budget travelers can plan a trip and book stays at discounted rates.



How To Get Thier


By Road : Somnath has better connectivity through roads. The road network connects Somnath with other major cities within and outside of Gujarat. There are frequent buses available from Ahmedabad, Jamnagar, Junagadh, Vadodara, and Surat. You can book private and government buses, both AC and non-AC to have a comfortable ride till Somnath. The road distance from Junagadh to Somnath is a mere 82 km. Ahmedabad is at 400 km away whereas Bhavnagar and Porbandar are at a distance of 270 and 120 km respectively. Tickets can be booked from any official ticket booking website.

Considering the religious importance of the city, Somnath has decent connectivity with other parts of Gujarat and India. The nearest railway station is Veraval, at about 6 km from Somnath. From Ahmedabad Junction, it is about 420 km and from Vadodara, it is at a distance of 530 km. There are direct trains available from Mumbai however from other metro cities like Delhi, Kolkata, and Bangalore, there are connecting trains which can take you to this city. You can visit the IRCTC website for checking ticket costing and other train related details.

Keshod is the nearest airport from Somnath situated at a distance of 50 km. There are regular flights available from Mumbai, Ahmedabad, and other major cities in India to Keshod. You can visit any official air ticket booking website. Check the price and book your tickets easily.

Some special occasions to visit the Somnath Temple

    Special occasions

  • Maha Shivratri: A festival celebrated with great pomp and splendor at the temple. It is usually celebrated in February or March.
  • Kartik Purnima: A festival celebrated near Diwali.
  • Shravan month: An auspicious time to visit the temple.

    • Other things to do

  • Attend the light and sound show: A captivating show that narrates the history of the temple.
  • Explore the surrounding areas: Visit the Triveni Sangam, where the Hiran, Kapila, and Saraswati rivers meet the Arabian Sea.
  • Visit the Bhalka Tirth: A place soaked in mythological significance, where Lord Krishna is said to have departed from this world.
