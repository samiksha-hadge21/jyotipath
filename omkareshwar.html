About Place:

It is situated in the Mandhata city (also known as Omkareshwar) in Khandwa district of Madhya Pradesh state in India. It is about 16 km from Barwaha in Madhya Pradesh. Omkareshwar is formed by the sacred river Narmada. This is one of the most sacred rivers in India and is now home to one of the world's largest solar power plant. The temple is situated on Mandhata or Shivpuri island on the banks of Narmada and Kaveri river (a tributary of Narmada). The island is 4 KM long and 2.6 km2 (2,600,000 m2) in area and can be approached by boats and bridge.


History:

The temple is said to have been built by the Paramara Kings of Malwa in the 11th century CE. After the Paramara kings, the administration of the temple was taken over by the Chauhan rulers. In the 13th century CE, the temple suffered destruction and loot by Muslim invaders starting with Mahmud Ghazni. Nevertheless, the temple remained intact without complete destruction. The temple was under the Chauhan Kings during the entire Mughal rule, without much renovation. In the 19th century, the temple was rebuilt by the Holkar rulers. The construction of the same was started by the 1st Holkar Queen,Gautama Bai Holkar and later completed by her daughter-in-law Devi Ahilyabai Holkar. The temple came under the British rule during the colonial era. After Independence in 1947, the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) took over the responsibility of the temple with the help of the Khandwa administration. According to the Shiva Purana, once, Brahma and Vishnu had an argument in terms of supremacy of creation.To test them, Shiva pierced the three worlds as a endless pillar of light, the jyotirlinga. Vishnu and Brahma split their ways to downwards and upwards respectively to find the end of the light in either directions. Brahma lied that he had discovered the end, while Vishnu conceded his defeat. Shiva appeared as the second pillar of light and cursed Brahma that he would have no place in ceremonies, while Vishnu would be worshipped until the end of eternity. The jyotirlinga shrines are believed to be those places where Shiva appeared as a fiery column of light. Originally, there were believed to be 64 jyotirlingas of which 12 of them are considered to be the holiest.

Architecture:

The Omkareshwar Jyotirlinga Temple in Khandwa, India is an example of Nagara-style architecture. This style is known for its tall spires, intricate sculptures, and detailed frescos. The temple was built using local sandstone and features a stone roof and verandas with carved columns. Features: Spires The temple has tall spires, or Shikharas Sculptures The temple is decorated with sculptures of gods, heavenly creatures, and scenes from knowledge Verandas The temple has large verandas with columns carved in squares, polygons, and circles Sabha Mandapam The temple has a spacious assembly hall, or Sabha Mandapam, supported by pillars Jyotirlinga The Jyotirlinga of Lord Shiva is installed in the sanctum sanctorum on the ground floor Shrines The temple has shrines for Panchamukhi Ganesha, Annapoorna Devi, Lord Karthikeya, and Goddess Parvati


Best Time To Visit

The peak season for visiting Omkareshwar extends from October to February, with temperatures ranging from approximately 15-30 degrees Celsius. This period is ideal for sightseeing, temple visits, and immersing yourself in the local culture.



How To Get Thier


By Road : Indore Indore, being a major city in Madhya Pradesh, serves as a primary hub for those traveling to Omkareshwar. State-run and private buses operate regular services between Indore and Omkareshwar. The distance between Indore and Omkareshwar is approximately 80 kilometers.

Nearest Railway Station The nearest railway station to Omkareshwar is Omkareshwar Road Railway Station (OMRD). While it is a small station, it connects to major railway junctions like Indore Junction and Khandwa Junction. Trains from cities like Indore and Bhopal operate to Omkareshwar Road Railway Station.

By air: The distance between Indore and Delhi is approximately 716 kilometers. Several airlines operate daily flights from Indira Gandhi International Airport (DEL) to Devi Ahilya Bai Holkar Airport (IDR) in Indore. The flight duration is around 2 hours.

Some special occasions to visit the omkareshwar Temple

    Special occasions

  • Maha Shivratri A festival celebrated during the Phalgun month of the Hindu calendar. The island is illuminated and pilgrims bathe in the Narmada River.
  • Makar Sankranti A harvest festival celebrated in January. People fly kites over the island.
  • Kartik Poornima A 10-day festival celebrated on the full moon day in November. Devotees bathe in the Narmada River and a fair is held.
  • Narmada Jayanti A festival dedicated to the Narmada River celebrated in the Magh month. The island is illuminated and fireworks are displayed.
  • Sawan The sacred month of Lord Shiva, which sees a large influx of tourists.

    • Other things to do

  • Take a boat ride on the Narmada River
  • Hire a priest or pandit for rituals
  • Visit the cave where Adi Shankara is said to have met his guru
  • Narmada Parikrama: A pilgrimage route that involves circling the entire Narmada River.
  • Pilgrimage tours: Visitors can take a dip in the Narmada River, perform religious rituals, and visit temples along the 7 km Parikrama.
